# Kirundi Honorifics, Titles & Ritual Politeness

- Shahu: Friendly, respectful title (younger/peer). “Bite ho, shahu!”
- Bossi: Borrowed from Swahili, urban sellers and delivery.
- Mukecuru/Mushasha: Elder woman/man, highly respectful (never use first names with elders).
- Mukenyezi: Madam, in business or government.
- Mweshimiwa: Honourable, for government/formal.
- Muganga: Doctor/professional. “Dawe Muganga” for medical context.
- Mugenzi: Fellow/peer in group chat settings.

## Decision Guide
| Context                | Typical Honorific         |
|------------------------|--------------------------|
| WhatsApp buyer/seller  | Shahu/Bossi              |
| Government/email       | Mweshimiwa/Mukenyezi     |
| Elders (in person)     | Mukecuru/Mushasha        |
| Healthcare            | Muganga                  |
