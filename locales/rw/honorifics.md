# Kinyarwanda Honorifics, Titles & Relationship Phrases

- **Boss:** Used in business and tech, neutral/friendly—works for both young and older clients (WhatsApp, IG, local marketplaces).
- **Afande:** Polite with police, officials, elders, or respected clients.
- **Muvandimwe:** "My brother/sister"—used to build friendly rapport without being too familiar (Rwandan culture, strong communal meaning).
- **Madamu/Shefu/Sir:** Formal, use in email or first business message if age/status is unknown.
- **Rwiyemezamirimo:** Entrepreneur/business owner (high respect).
- **Umuyobozi:** Manager or leader, especially government.
- **Sha:** Only for peers (never elders) and never in professional opening.

## Decision Table
| Context                              | Recommended Title   |
|--------------------------------------|---------------------|
| WhatsApp/marketplace, unknown client | Boss                |
| Elder/formal, government/police      | Afande              |
| Friends/peers, casual                | Sha, Muvandimwe     |
| Email/business, unknown              | Madamu/Sir          |

_Always err on the side of respect and warmth in business chats._
