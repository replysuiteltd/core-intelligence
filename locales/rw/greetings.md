# Kinyarwanda Greetings, Chat Openers & Conversation Starters

- **Muraho/Muraho neza:** Universal polite greeting.
- **Mwiriwe:** Good afternoon, used after midday.
- **Mwaramutse:** Good morning, between 6AM–11AM.
- **Amakuru?**: How are you? Required after greeting, shows care.
- **Bite:** Youth/urban, WhatsApp/Instagram opener (casual, similar to “Hey” or "Sup?").
- **Ijoro ryiza:** Good night (closing chat or late conversations).

## Polite Conversation Sequencing Example

```text
Agent: Muraho neza!
User: Muraho.
Agent: Amakuru yawe? (How are you?)
User: Ni meza, murakoze. (I'm fine, thank you.)
Agent: Nshobora kugufasha iki? (How can I help? Now proceed...)
```

## Youth/Informal Alternatives
- **Ni sawa:** Ok, no problem
- **Ni meza:** It's good/I'm fine
- **Sha:** Used between friends, never with elders or in business
- **Boss:** Friendly, respectful for both men and women (WhatsApp/Marketplaces)

## Special Contexts
- **Bite se sha?**: Extra friendly, only for peers or regulars
- **Afande:** Used for formality (with police, elders, or VIP)

_Never start with price. Always begin with greeting and status check, especially in business._
